Project Galileo III: A Desert Challenge

A showcase of GPS International's expertise in heavy transport and logistical innovation through one of our most challenging desert operations.

Rig Galileo III Transport Operation

Transport of heavy equipment for Baker Hughes Rig Galileo III project through the challenging Algerian desert terrain.

The Challenge

Project Galileo III was a monumental challenge that proved to be a resounding success. GPS International mobilized its full expertise and resources to transport heavy equipment for Baker Hughes Rig Galileo III through the challenging Algerian desert terrain.

Project Scope & Achievements

Transport Operation Door-to-Door Heavy Equipment
Oversized Material 37m x 10m x 10m = 165 Tons
Transport Distance Over 450 km
Obstacles Overcome 358 obstacles (125 electric lines)
Team Size 80 persons involved
Project Timeline 2.5 months preparation + 14 days transport

Key Project Highlights

  • Transport of heavy and over-sized equipment over more than 450 km from the Zarzis port to the Algerian town of Rhourde El Baguel
  • Development and construction of more than 30 km of new roads in the desert
  • Transformed a long journey into a 14-day drive through the desert
  • Comprehensive obstacle management including 125 electric lines
  • Meticulous planning and execution of convoy transportation

The Outcome

Despite the challenging desert terrain and numerous obstacles, Project Galileo III was completed successfully within the planned timeline. The construction of 30 km of new desert roads turned what would have been an impossible journey into a 14-day success story, demonstrating GPS International's capability to overcome even the most difficult logistical challenges.

Project Overview

GPS International put all its power into this project to make every minute of it worth the effort. Transport and road development were the key elements of the project, which demonstrated our unparalleled capabilities in desert logistics and heavy transport.

The project involved the transport of Baker Hughes' Rig Galileo III components from Zarzis port to the remote Algerian desert location of Rhourde El Baguel, covering over 450 km of challenging terrain with extreme weather conditions.

Project Scope & Services

Door-to-Door Transport Complete transport of heavy and oversized equipment
Oversized Material Handling 37m x 10m x 10m = 165 Tons total weight
Desert Route Development Construction of 30+ km of new desert roads
Obstacle Management 358 obstacles including 125 electric lines overcome
Transport Distance Over 450 km from Zarzis port to Rhourde El Baguel
Team Coordination 80-person team working in coordination
Project Timeline 2.5 months preparation + 14 days desert transport
Convoy Planning Meticulous convoy transportation planning
Safety Management Comprehensive safety protocols for desert operations
Route Survey & Planning Detailed route survey and contingency planning
Equipment Maintenance On-site maintenance and repair capabilities
Communication Systems Satellite communication for remote desert operations
